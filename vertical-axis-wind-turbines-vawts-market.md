<p><p>Vertical-axis Wind Turbines (VAWTs) come in different market types, such as Darrieus and Savonius turbines. Darrieus turbines have a curved design with large blades, allowing them to capture wind from any direction. They are efficient and can generate high power outputs. Savonius turbines, on the other hand, have a simpler design with vertical blades that rotate due to drag forces. They are more suitable for lower wind speeds and are often used in small-scale applications. Both types offer unique advantages and cater to different market needs.</p></p>
