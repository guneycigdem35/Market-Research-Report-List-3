<p><p>A heart valve prosthesis is an artificial device used to replace a defective or diseased heart valve. These prosthetic valves are used in patients who suffer from conditions such as valvular stenosis or regurgitation, where the natural valve does not function properly. There are two main types of heart valve prostheses - mechanical valves made of durable materials like metal or carbon, and biological valves made from animal or human tissue.</p><p>The heart valve prosthesis market is projected to experience significant growth in the coming years, with a forecasted compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.9%. This growth can be attributed to factors such as the increasing prevalence of heart valve diseases, advancements in heart valve replacement technologies, and a growing aging population. Additionally, the rising demand for minimally invasive procedures and improved surgical techniques are driving the market expansion.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the heart valve prosthesis market include the development of transcatheter heart valve replacements, which offer a less invasive alternative to traditional open-heart surgery.
