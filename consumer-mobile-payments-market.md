<p><p>Consumer mobile payments can be categorized into two main types: remote and proximity markets. Remote payments involve transactions made through mobile devices without physically being present at the point of sale. This includes online shopping or bill payments. On the other hand, proximity payments involve transactions made in person, typically using Near Field Communication (NFC) technology. This includes tap-and-go payments at stores or restaurants. Both markets are growing rapidly due to the convenience and speed of mobile payments.</p></p>
