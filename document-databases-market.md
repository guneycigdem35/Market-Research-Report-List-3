<p><p>Document databases come in several market types, including key-value, column-oriented, document stored, and graph-based databases. Key-value databases store data with a unique key that is used for retrieval. Column-oriented databases store data in columns rather than rows to optimize read performance. Document stored databases store data in flexible, JSON-like documents for easy organization. Graph-based databases focus on relationships between data points, making them ideal for complex data structures. Each market type has its own strengths and is designed for specific use cases.</p></p>
