<p><p>Waste to Energy (WTE) technologies can be categorized into two main types: thermal technologies and biochemical reactions. Thermal technologies involve the combustion of waste to generate heat, which is then converted into electricity. Biochemical reactions, on the other hand, involve the breakdown of organic waste materials through biological processes to produce biogas or biofuels. Both types of WTE technologies offer solutions for converting waste materials into energy, thus reducing the burden on landfills and contributing to a more sustainable energy future.</p></p>
