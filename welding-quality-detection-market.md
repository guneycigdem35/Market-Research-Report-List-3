<p><p>Welding quality detection involves assessing the integrity of welds to ensure they meet required standards. Destructive detection methods involve physically testing samples by cutting or breaking them to assess weld quality. Non-destructive detection methods examine welds without altering the sample, using techniques like ultrasound or x-ray. Both types of detection play crucial roles in ensuring welds are strong and defect-free, with destructive methods providing in-depth analysis and non-destructive methods offering a less intrusive way to assess weld quality.</p></p>
