<p><p>Forged crankshafts are classified into two main types based on their manufacturing process: Integral and Assembled. Integral crankshafts are made from a single piece of metal, providing superior strength and durability. They are usually used in high-performance engines where reliability is crucial. On the other hand, Assembled crankshafts are made by assembling multiple pieces together through welding or other methods. They are typically used in less demanding applications where cost is a significant factor. Each type of crankshaft has its advantages and limitations depending on the specific requirements of the engine.</p></p>
