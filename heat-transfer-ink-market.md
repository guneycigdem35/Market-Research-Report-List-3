<p><p>Heat transfer ink can be classified into various types based on the different mechanisms of transfer and fixation on the substrate. Disperse ink is commonly used for transferring designs onto polyester fabrics as it sublimates under heat and bonds with the fibers. Reactive ink is used for natural fibers like cotton, as it chemically reacts with the fabric to create a durable bond. There are other types of heat transfer inks available in the market, each designed for specific substrates and application methods.</p></p>
