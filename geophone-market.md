<p><p>Geophones are commonly categorized into four main types: Electric Geophones, which use electromagnetic principles to detect seismic waves; Piezoelectric Geophones, which convert mechanical vibrations into electrical signals; Eddy Current Geophones, which use changes in magnetic fields to detect seismic waves; and Digital Geophones, which utilize advanced digital technology for data recording and processing. Each type of geophone has its own advantages and applications in various industries such as oil and gas exploration, earthquake monitoring, and geotechnical engineering.</p></p>
