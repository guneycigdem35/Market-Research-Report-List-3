There are four main types of mucoactive agents: expectorants, which help to cough up mucus; mucolytics, which break down thick mucus; mucokinetics, which help to move mucus along the respiratory tract; and mucoregulators, which help to regulate the production of mucus. These agents are commonly used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, COPD, and cystic fibrosis.</p></p>
