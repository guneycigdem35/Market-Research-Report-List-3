<p><p>Biomedical textiles are categorized into three main types - non-woven textiles, woven textiles, and knitted textiles. Non-woven textiles are made from fibers that are bonded together through mechanical, chemical, or thermal processes. Woven textiles are created by interlacing yarns in a crisscross pattern, resulting in a strong and durable fabric. Knitted textiles are produced by looping yarns together, creating a more flexible and stretchable material. Each type of biomedical textile offers unique properties and applications within the medical and healthcare industries.</p></p>
