<p><p>Open lifesaving watercraft are boats without a cabin or enclosed structure, providing easy access to the water for rescues. This type is commonly used for lifeguards on beaches or in open water. On the other hand, semi-enclosed lifesaving watercraft have a partially enclosed structure, offering some protection from the elements while still allowing for quick deployment into the water. These types are often used in rougher waters or colder climates where shelter is necessary for the safety of both rescuers and victims.</p></p>
