<p><p>GPS tracking devices are categorized into two main market types: satellite-based and cellular-based. Satellite-based GPS devices rely on satellites to acquire location data, making them ideal for tracking in remote areas without cellular coverage. On the other hand, cellular-based GPS devices use cellular networks to transmit location data in real-time, providing a more cost-effective and efficient solution for urban areas with reliable network coverage. Both types of GPS tracking devices offer unique advantages depending on the specific tracking needs of the user.</p></p>
