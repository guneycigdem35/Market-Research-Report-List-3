<p><p>Linear piezoelectric motors are classified into three types based on the materials used for the piezoelectric elements: ceramics, composites, and polymers. Ceramics offer high stiffness and excellent stability, making them ideal for precision applications. Composites combine the properties of ceramics and polymers, offering a balance between performance and flexibility. Polymers are elastic and lightweight, suitable for applications requiring high speed and low force. Each type of motor has its unique advantages and is chosen based on the specific requirements of the application.</p></p>
