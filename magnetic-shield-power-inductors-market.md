<p><p>Magnetic shield power inductors come in two main types: Multilayer power inductors and wirewound power inductors. Multilayer power inductors are made up of multiple layers of conductor coils, offering high inductance and efficiency in a compact size. Wirewound power inductors, on the other hand, are coil structures made of wire wound around a magnetic core, providing high current-handling capabilities. Both types of power inductors are essential components in electronic devices for filtering noise, storing energy, and regulating voltage.</p></p>
