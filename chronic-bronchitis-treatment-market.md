<p><p>Chronic bronchitis is typically treated with a combination of antibiotics, anti-inflammatory drugs, bronchodilators, and mucolytics. Antibiotics are used to treat bacterial infections that may be causing the bronchitis. Anti-inflammatory drugs help reduce inflammation in the airways, while bronchodilators help to open up the airways, making it easier to breathe. Mucolytics are medications that help to thin mucus in the airways, making it easier to cough up. These treatments work together to manage symptoms and improve quality of life for patients with chronic bronchitis.</p></p>
